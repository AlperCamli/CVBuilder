ATS Resume Template: A Simple Structure You Can Use Today

Use this simple ATS resume template to create a clean, readable CV with a clear summary, skills, experience, education, certifications, and projects.

Updated May 28, 2026

A resume does not need to be complicated to be effective.

In fact, when you apply online, simple is often better.

Many companies use applicant tracking systems, also called ATS, to collect and organize resumes. These systems may scan your CV for contact details, work experience, skills, education, dates, certifications, and keywords from the job description.

If your resume uses a complicated design, important information may be harder to read.

That is why an ATS resume template should be clean, structured, and easy to scan.

In this guide, you will get a simple ATS-friendly resume template you can use today, plus tips for writing each section clearly.

What is an ATS resume template?

An ATS resume template is a resume structure designed to be easy for applicant tracking systems and recruiters to read.

It usually avoids:

  • Complex tables
  • Heavy graphics
  • Text boxes
  • Icons used instead of words
  • Multiple columns
  • Images containing important text
  • Unusual section headings
  • Decorative skill bars

Instead, it uses:

  • Standard section names
  • Plain text
  • Clear dates
  • Simple bullet points
  • Consistent spacing
  • A logical order
  • Relevant keywords
  • Easy-to-read formatting

The goal is not to make your resume boring.

The goal is to make your experience clear.

A clean template helps your skills, achievements, and qualifications stand out without being hidden behind design. It should follow an ATS-friendly resume format.

Simple ATS resume template

Use this structure as your base:

  • FIRSTNAME LASTNAME
  • Email | Phone | City, Country | LinkedIn | Portfolio or Website
  • PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY: A short 2-4 line summary focused on your target role, relevant experience, key skills, and strongest value.
  • SKILLS: Skill 1 | Skill 2 | Skill 3 | Skill 4 | Skill 5 | Tool 1 | Tool 2 | Methodology
  • WORK EXPERIENCE
  • Job Title, Company Name | City, Country | Month Year - Month Year
  • Bullet point showing a relevant responsibility, action, and result.
  • Bullet point using keywords from the job description naturally.
  • Bullet point showing a measurable achievement or clear business impact.
  • Bullet point showing collaboration, tools, or role-specific experience.
  • EDUCATION: Degree or Program, School Name | City, Country | Graduation Year
  • CERTIFICATIONS: Certification Name | Provider | Year
  • PROJECTS: Project Name | Tool, Skill, or Context
  • Short bullet point explaining what you built, improved, analyzed, managed, or delivered.

This template works because it is simple, readable, and easy to customize.

Why this ATS resume template works

This structure works well because it puts the most important information where recruiters expect to find it.

Your contact information appears at the top.

Your summary explains your fit.

Your skills section makes key qualifications easy to scan.

Your work experience proves your value.

Your education, certifications, and projects support your application.

It also uses standard headings, which can help both recruiters and hiring systems understand the document.

A good template should not force the reader to figure out where everything is.

It should make your experience easy to follow.

Section 1: Contact information

Your contact information should be simple and clear.

Use this format:

FIRSTNAME LASTNAME, email@example.com | +90 555 555 55 55 | Istanbul, Turkey | linkedin.com/in/name | portfolio.com

Include:

  • Full name
  • Professional email
  • Phone number
  • City and country
  • LinkedIn profile, if updated
  • Portfolio, GitHub, or website, if relevant

Avoid:

  • Full home address
  • Multiple phone numbers
  • Unprofessional email addresses
  • Icons without text
  • Contact details inside images
  • Broken links

Your contact information should be written as normal text so it is easy to copy, read, and parse.

Section 2: Professional summary

Your professional summary should quickly answer:

Why are you a good fit for this role?

Keep it short. Two to four lines are enough.

A weak summary sounds generic:

Motivated and hardworking professional with strong communication skills and a passion for success.

A stronger summary is specific:

Customer success specialist with experience supporting customer onboarding, resolving account issues, maintaining CRM records, and improving product adoption through clear communication and follow-up.

The second summary is better because it includes role-specific information.

It tells the recruiter what kind of work you have done and why it matters.

This is also where you can tailor your resume to the job description quickly.

Section 3: Skills

Your skills section should be focused on the job you are applying for.

Do not list every skill you have.

List the most relevant skills, tools, and methods from the job description that match your real experience.

Example for a marketing role:

SEO | Content strategy | Google Analytics | Keyword research | Campaign reporting | Email marketing | Conversion optimization

Example for a project coordinator role:

Project coordination | Stakeholder communication | Jira | Task tracking | Reporting | Documentation | Agile workflows

Example for a customer success role:

Customer onboarding | CRM | Account management | Issue resolution | Product adoption | Client communication | Retention support

Your skills section should be easy to scan.

It should also connect to your work experience. If you list a skill, try to show evidence of that skill in your bullet points. This is where resume keywords matter.

Section 4: Work experience

Your work experience section is usually the most important part of your resume.

Use this structure:

  • Job Title
  • Company Name | City, Country | Month Year - Month Year
  • Action verb + task + context + result.
  • Action verb + keyword or tool + responsibility + outcome.
  • Action verb + collaboration + business impact.

For example:

Marketing Specialist, Brightline Media | Istanbul, Turkey | March 2022 - April 2025

  • Planned SEO-focused blog content using keyword research, search intent analysis, and performance data.
  • Managed campaign calendars across blog, email, and social media channels to improve launch consistency.
  • Prepared monthly marketing reports summarizing traffic, engagement, and conversion trends.
  • Collaborated with sales and product teams to align campaign messaging with customer needs.

This format is strong because each bullet point is specific.

It shows what the candidate did, how they did it, and why it mattered.

How to write ATS-friendly bullet points

A strong resume bullet point should be clear, specific, and relevant.

Use this formula:

Action + task + tool/context + result

Examples:

Weak: Responsible for reports.

Better: Prepared weekly performance reports using CRM data to help the sales team track pipeline progress.

Weak: Helped customers.

Better: Supported customer onboarding by answering setup questions, resolving account issues, and documenting recurring feedback.

Weak: Worked on marketing.

Better: Planned SEO content updates using keyword research and Google Analytics data to improve organic visibility.

Good bullet points help both ATS systems and recruiters because they include relevant terms in a natural way.

Section 5: Education

Your education section can be simple.

Use this format:

EDUCATION: Bachelor of Science in Business Administration, Marmara University | Istanbul, Turkey | 2023

Include:

  • Degree or program name
  • School name
  • Location, optional
  • Graduation year, if relevant
  • Relevant coursework, if you are early career
  • Honors, if useful

If you are a student or recent graduate, education can appear above work experience.

If you have several years of relevant experience, education usually belongs below work experience.

Section 6: Certifications

Certifications can help if they are relevant to the role.

Use this format:

  • Google Analytics Certification | Google | 2025
  • Project Management Fundamentals | Coursera | 2024

Include certifications that are:

  • Required in the job description
  • Relevant to your target role
  • Recognized in your industry
  • Connected to a skill you want to highlight

Avoid listing outdated or unrelated certificates that distract from the role.

Section 7: Projects

Projects are useful if they show relevant skills.

They are especially helpful for:

  • Students
  • Recent graduates
  • Career changers
  • Software engineers
  • Data analysts
  • Designers
  • Marketers
  • Product candidates
  • Freelancers

Use this format:

PROJECTS: Sales Dashboard Project | Excel, Tableau, Data Analysis

Built a dashboard to track weekly sales performance, identify pipeline trends, and summarize insights for decision-making.

A good project entry should explain what you made, which tools you used, what problem it solved, and what result or learning came from it.

Keep it short and relevant.

Should your ATS resume be one page or two pages?

There is no perfect length for everyone.

A one-page resume may be best if you are:

  • A student
  • A recent graduate
  • Early in your career
  • Applying for entry-level roles
  • Changing careers and focusing only on transferable experience

A two-page resume may be better if you have:

  • Several years of relevant experience
  • Multiple roles with strong achievements
  • Technical projects
  • Leadership experience
  • Certifications
  • Specialized skills

The goal is not to make your resume short.

The goal is to make it focused.

Do not cut useful content just to reach one page. But do not keep weak or irrelevant content just to fill space.

ATS resume template for students or recent graduates

If you have limited work experience, use this structure:

  • FIRSTNAME LASTNAME
  • Email | Phone | City, Country | LinkedIn | Portfolio
  • PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY: Short summary focused on your target role, relevant coursework, projects, internships, and skills.
  • SKILLS: Skill 1 | Skill 2 | Tool 1 | Tool 2 | Methodology
  • EDUCATION: Degree or Program, School Name | City, Country | Graduation Year
  • PROJECTS: Project Name | Tools or Skills
  • EXPERIENCE: Job Title or Internship, Company Name | Dates
  • CERTIFICATIONS: Certification Name | Provider | Year

For early-career candidates, projects and education may be more important than work history.

The key is to show practical ability, not just coursework.

ATS resume template for career changers

If you are changing careers, use this structure:

  • FIRSTNAME LASTNAME
  • Email | Phone | City, Country | LinkedIn | Portfolio
  • PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY: Short summary connecting your previous experience to your target role.
  • SKILLS: Transferable Skill 1 | Role-specific Skill 1 | Tool 1 | Communication | Analysis | Project coordination
  • RELEVANT EXPERIENCE: Job Title, Company Name | Dates
  • PROJECTS OR TRAINING: Project, Course, or Certification
  • ADDITIONAL EXPERIENCE: Older or less relevant role
  • EDUCATION: Degree or Program, School Name | Year

For career changers, the summary and skills sections are especially important.

They help explain the connection between where you have been and where you want to go. A job-specific resume makes that connection clearer.

Common ATS template mistakes to avoid

Avoid these mistakes when using an ATS resume template.

Mistake 1: Choosing design over clarity

A beautiful template is not useful if it hides your experience.

Mistake 2: Using two columns for important content

Two-column layouts can look modern, but they may create reading-order issues.

Mistake 3: Adding skill bars

Skill bars are vague and not very useful.

Instead of showing "Excel: 90%," list Excel as a skill and prove it in your experience.

Mistake 4: Using icons instead of words

Icons can be misunderstood or ignored.

Use text for important information.

Mistake 5: Keeping the same template content for every job

The template can stay the same, but the content should change based on the role. Use a resume checklist before applying.

How to customize this template for each job

The structure can stay consistent, but the content should be tailored.

Before each application:

  • Read the job description.
  • Identify the most important keywords.
  • Update your professional summary.
  • Reorder your skills.
  • Rewrite your most relevant bullet points.
  • Remove or shorten unrelated details.
  • Check the format and file name.

This usually takes much less time than rewriting your resume from scratch.

The template gives you the structure.

The job description tells you what to emphasize.

Final thoughts

An ATS resume template should make your experience easier to understand.

It does not need heavy design, graphics, or complicated formatting.

Use a simple structure: contact information, professional summary, skills, work experience, education, certifications, and projects.

Keep your headings clear. Use relevant keywords naturally. Write bullet points that show real experience. Avoid design elements that make your CV harder to parse.

The best resume template is not the most decorative one.

It is the one that helps your qualifications get noticed.
