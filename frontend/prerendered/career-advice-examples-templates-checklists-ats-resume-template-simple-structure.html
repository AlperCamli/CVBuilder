ATS Resume Template: A Simple Structure You Can Use Today

A clean ATS resume template with section order, example wording, and practical notes for keeping the document easy to scan.

Updated June 30, 2026

Use the template with a real job description.

A simple structure works best when the content is matched to the role.

Create my tailored CV

This template is intentionally simple. It is designed for applications where clarity matters more than decoration: ATS parsing, recruiter scanning, and easy tailoring.

The ATS resume guide explains the concept; this page gives you the structure. Copy the order into your own document, then use the resume checklist before you apply.

Template

  • Name | Email | Phone | City | LinkedIn or portfolio
  • Professional Summary: 2-3 lines focused on role fit.
  • Skills: grouped tools, methods, clinical areas, or technical strengths.
  • Work Experience: job title, employer, dates, then 3-5 bullets for recent roles.
  • Education: degree, institution, dates or graduation year.
  • Certifications: licenses, courses, registrations, or credentials.
  • Projects, publications, audits, or volunteering if relevant.

Example summary

Operations coordinator with 5 years of experience supporting customer onboarding, reporting, and cross-functional project tracking. Strong background in Excel dashboards, CRM data quality, and weekly stakeholder updates.

Example experience block

Operations Coordinator, Northline Software, 2023-Present

  • Maintained onboarding trackers for 40+ active customer accounts, flagging blockers before weekly implementation calls.
  • Built Excel reporting templates used by support and sales teams to monitor renewal risk.
  • Updated CRM records after customer meetings, improving handover quality between sales and onboarding.

Template rules

A good ATS resume template should feel plain in the right way. The value is not visual novelty; it is a section order that lets a recruiter find job titles, dates, skills, and evidence without effort.

RuleWhy it helps
Use one main columnThe reading order stays clear.
Keep headings plainRecruiters and ATS software recognize the sections.
Write bullets as evidenceThe CV feels specific instead of generic.
Tailor before exportingThe final version matches the role, not every possible job.

Adapt the template by seniority

Early-career candidates can include projects, internships, coursework, volunteering, and placements if they prove relevant skills. Senior candidates should spend less space on early education and more on recent scope, leadership, commercial impact, clinical responsibility, or technical ownership.

For medical or regulated roles, add registration and mandatory credentials near the top. For technical roles, group tools by type so the skills section does not become an unreadable list.

For job-specific applications, update the template after reading the job description. The same layout can support different versions as long as the summary, skills, and first bullets change with the role.
