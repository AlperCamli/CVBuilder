NHS Doctor CV Example: Sections, Skills and Structure

A compact NHS doctor CV structure with examples for clinical experience, audits, teaching, leadership, and courses.

Updated June 30, 2026

A doctor CV works best when it separates eligibility from evidence. Put registration, grade, and courses near the top, then use the rest of the CV to show clinical judgement, communication, teaching, and improvement work.

This structure is designed for NHS doctor applications. For roles outside the UK, compare it with the broader medical CV guide so the terminology, registration details, and section order match the employer.

Recommended section order

  • Name, contact details, GMC status.
  • Short profile naming grade, specialty interest, and strongest fit.
  • Certifications and courses, including ALS if required.
  • Clinical experience in reverse chronological order.
  • Audit, QI, research, publications, and presentations.
  • Teaching, leadership, management, and memberships.
  • Education and qualifications.

Example profile

GMC-registered doctor with acute medicine and emergency department experience, confident with ward reviews, patient documentation, escalation, and multidisciplinary handovers. Interested in clinical governance and service improvement, with recent audit work on discharge summary quality.

Write clinical bullets with context

WeakStronger
Worked in acute medicine and saw patients.Reviewed acute medical admissions under senior supervision, documenting plans, requesting investigations, and escalating deterioration.
Did audits and teaching.Completed an audit of discharge summary delays, presented findings at departmental teaching, and helped introduce a ward checklist.
Good team player.Coordinated with nursing, pharmacy, and therapy teams during ward rounds to support discharge planning.

What to tailor for each post

For a trust-grade medicine post, move acute ward experience and ALS higher. For a teaching-heavy role, bring teaching and feedback forward. For a specialty training application, show portfolio evidence, QI, publications, presentations, and specialty commitment.

Do not let extra sections crowd out clinical fit

Doctors often have many possible sections: prizes, conferences, memberships, electives, leadership roles, courses, and publications. Include them, but keep the strongest clinical evidence easy to find. A recruiter should not have to pass three pages of extras before seeing your recent post and responsibilities.

If an achievement is old or only loosely related, shorten it to one line. Save the detail for applications where that evidence is central.

Before exporting, check the basics: dates are consistent, GMC status is accurate, courses are current, and each section supports the post. The resume checklist is not medical-specific, but it is useful for avoidable mistakes.
