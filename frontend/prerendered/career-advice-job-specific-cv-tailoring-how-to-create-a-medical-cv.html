How to Write a Medical CV for UK and US Healthcare Jobs

A concise medical CV guide covering clinical experience, registration, education, audits, teaching, research, and role-specific tailoring.

Updated June 30, 2026

Tailor your medical CV to the post.

Use the job description to choose the clinical evidence, courses, audits, and skills that matter for this application.

Tailor my medical CV

A medical CV has to do more than list jobs. It needs to show clinical safety, scope of practice, training, and evidence that you can work inside the system you are applying to.

The exact format changes between the UK and US, but the reader still wants the same thing: accurate credentials, relevant clinical experience, and a clear fit for the post.

The CV vs resume distinction matters most when you are crossing countries or systems. For nursing applications, the NHS nurse CV checklist gives a more role-specific version of the same principles.

Put credentials where they cannot be missed

  • Name and contact details.
  • Professional registration, such as GMC, NMC, HCPC, state license, or board eligibility where relevant.
  • Current role, grade, specialty, and main setting.
  • Right to work or visa status if the employer asks for it.
  • Key certifications such as ALS, BLS, ACLS, PALS, or specialty-specific training.

Choose sections based on the role

If applying forEmphasize
NHS doctor postGMC status, grade, rotations, audits, teaching, courses, portfolio evidence
Nursing roleNMC status, ward or specialty experience, medication safety, handovers, patient documentation
US clinical roleLicense, board status, residency or fellowship, procedural volume, patient population
Research or academic rolePublications, presentations, grants, methods, ethics approvals, teaching

Write clinical experience with scope

Avoid vague lines such as worked in a busy hospital. Say where you worked, who you cared for, what you were trusted to do, and how you communicated risk.

  • Managed daily reviews for acute medical patients under consultant supervision, including documentation, escalation, and discharge planning.
  • Supported post-operative patients on a surgical ward, monitoring observations and escalating deterioration according to local protocol.
  • Completed QI work on discharge summaries, auditing delays and presenting changes at departmental teaching.

UK and US differences

UK medical CVs often include clinical rotations, audits, teaching, courses, and portfolio activity. US applications may lean more on residency, fellowship, board status, publications, procedural exposure, and formal credentials. Do not send the same version to both without adjusting terminology.

Make dates, gaps, and scope easy to understand

Healthcare CVs are checked carefully. Keep dates consistent, explain career breaks briefly when needed, and avoid making a short observership sound like a full clinical post. If you changed country or system, make the transition clear so the reader understands what experience is directly transferable.

Scope matters too. A line about emergency medicine means more when it says whether you clerked patients, assisted procedures, completed documentation, joined ward rounds, or worked under direct supervision.

A medical CV can be detailed, but it still needs a final resume checklist for missing dates, unclear registration details, and formatting issues.

NHS-specific applications need the NHS medical CV guide's section order. Doctor roles can follow the doctor CV formula when clinical grade, audits, and teaching need to lead.
